# Orbace Sudocoo - User Help Guide

## 1. Home Screen

When you open Orbace Sudocoo, you land on the Home screen — your starting point for everything.

From here you can:

- Play today's Tea Moment.
- Browse Level Packs.
- Import a puzzle of your own.
- Visit Record Hall to look back on puzzles you've completed.
- Check your progress on Scholar's Path or Extreme Challenge, once they're unlocked.

You may also see a small ad banner here to help keep the app free. Home is meant to feel calm and simple — a quiet place to decide what to play next.

## 2. Tea Moment

Tea Moment is your daily puzzle — one calm, unhurried Sudocoo each day.

一局一茶 means One Puzzle, One Tea: one calm puzzle session, one quiet tea moment.

To play:

1. Open the app.
2. Tap Tea Moment.
3. Solve today's puzzle at your own pace.

Everything you'd expect from a Sudocoo board is here: enter numbers, jot notes, erase, undo and redo, ask for a hint, pause, and check for mistakes. Your progress saves automatically, so you can step away and pick up right where you left off. When you finish, it's saved as a Su-Pu — Orbace's name for a completed, saved solve record you can revisit anytime.

Tea Moment is great for a quick daily habit or a relaxed warm-up before something harder.

## 3. Level Packs

Level Packs hold the full Orbace puzzle library, organized by difficulty so you can work your way up.

To play:

1. From Home, tap Level Packs.
2. Pick a difficulty or pack.
3. Choose a puzzle.
4. Play.

As you complete puzzles, you'll see a marker next to them so it's easy to track what's left, and your progress through each pack is saved automatically. Solve one cleanly and it can also count toward your local ranking — more on that below.

Level Packs are the best path if you want steady, structured progress through Orbace's catalog.

## 4. Import a Puzzle

Have a Sudoku-style puzzle from somewhere else you'd like to solve in Orbace? Import Puzzle lets you bring it in.

To import:

1. From Home or Level Packs, tap Import Puzzle.
2. Give it a name, if you like.
3. Enter it by pasting an 81-character string or filling in the grid by hand.
4. Preview it to make sure it looks right.
5. Tap Save & Play.

A couple of things to know: it needs to be a valid 9x9 Sudocoo with exactly one solution, and you can leave empty cells blank, enter them as zero, or mark them with a dot.

Here's an example string you can try:

530070000600195000098000060800060003400803001700020006060000280000419005000080079

Imported puzzles are yours to play, replay, rate, and share. Since they come from outside Orbace, though, they won't count toward official rankings unless Orbace reviews and certifies them later.

## 5. Completion Certificate

Finish a puzzle, and Orbace hands you a completion certificate — your personal record of that solve.

It shows the puzzle name and difficulty, your final score and score class, how long you took, and details like steps, mistakes, hints used, and accuracy — plus whether the solve was clean or assisted, and a plain-language explanation of how your score was worked out. You can add your own difficulty rating and notes, right away or later.

Think of the certificate as your solve's report card — a quick, satisfying summary the moment you finish.

## 6. Save & Share Your Card

Want to show off a solve? Turn your completion certificate into a shareable image.

Right after finishing:

1. On the completion certificate, tap Save Card to keep the image on your device.
2. Tap Share Card to send it wherever you like, through your device's normal share menu.

You can also come back to any past solve later:

1. Open Record Hall.
2. Pick the puzzle you want.
3. Open its saved card.
4. Share it again anytime.

Every card stays on your device and carries Orbace's own look and feel.

## 7. Record Hall

Record Hall is your personal library of everything you've completed.

To browse:

1. From Home, tap Record Hall.
2. Look through your completed puzzles.
3. Use search or mark favorites to find things faster.
4. Open any record to replay it, see the details, view or share its card, or add notes.

Want to clear one out? You can delete a record — this removes that attempt's replay, score, and notes, but leaves the puzzle itself and your other attempts at it untouched.

Record Hall is where your Sudocoo history lives — come back anytime to relive a solve or see how far you've come.

## 8. Replay

Curious how you actually solved a puzzle? Replay walks you through it, move by move.

To watch a replay:

1. Open it from the completion certificate, Record Hall, or Su-Pu Detail.
2. Use the controls to step forward and backward through your solve.
3. Watch each number you entered, every note you jotted, and any undos along the way.

Replay is a great way to learn from your own solves, spot where a mistake crept in, or see how your approach to a puzzle changed over time.

## 9. Su-Pu Detail

Solved the same puzzle more than once? Su-Pu Detail brings everything you've done with it into one place.

To open it:

1. Go to Record Hall.
2. Select a completed puzzle.
3. Tap Su-Pu Detail.

Here you'll find your most recent solve, your best official and overall results, and how you've improved across attempts, with quick access to replays, saved cards, and a retry button. Solved it at least twice? You can also compare two attempts side by side.

It's the best place to see your own progress on a puzzle you keep coming back to.

## 10. Local Ranking

Local Ranking answers a simple question: of all your solves for this puzzle, which one was your best?

It ranks your attempts by score, time, mistakes, and hints — but only solves that qualify. To count toward ranking, a solve generally needs to be a built-in Orbace puzzle, completed without hints or auto-check, and not a disqualified retry.

A couple of things worth knowing: imported puzzles don't count toward ranking, and assisted solves are still saved and shareable — they just won't show up as a ranked result. Right now, ranking is based only on your own device.

Not sure whether to use Local Ranking or Compare Su-Pu? Local Ranking automatically surfaces your best eligible solve, leaderboard-style. Compare Su-Pu is more hands-on — you pick two solves yourself to see exactly what changed between them.

## 11. Compare Su-Pu / 对谱

Compare Su-Pu lets you set two of your solves for the same puzzle side by side to see how they differ.

To compare:

1. Solve the same puzzle at least twice.
2. Open Record Hall, then Su-Pu Detail for that puzzle.
3. Tap Compare Su-Pu / 对谱.
4. Pick the two attempts you want to compare.

You'll see the difference in score, time, steps, mistakes, hints, accuracy, score class, and clean status — a clear picture of what changed between two solves.

## 12. Scholar's Path and Extreme Challenge

Scholar's Path tracks your progress through three stages — Foundation, Discipline, and Insight — as you build up your Sudocoo skills.

Complete Insight, and you'll unlock Extreme Challenge: a tougher, no-assist puzzle for players who are ready for it. Daily Extreme Challenge draws from the Expert Challenge pack's puzzles. To have an Extreme Challenge attempt count toward ranking, solve it without hints, auto-check, or retries.

## 13. About the Ads

Orbace Sudocoo is free to play, and a small ad banner may appear on Home and other screens — including during gameplay — to help keep it that way.

## 14. Settings

You'll find Settings behind the gear icon on the Home screen. From there you can:

- Manage your ad personalization choices, if that option is available in your region.
- Review our Terms of Use.
- Check the app's version and copyright info under About.
- Come back to this guide, or reach out for support, under Help.
